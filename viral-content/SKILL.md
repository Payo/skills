# Viral Content

A research-backed system for developing social content with a real shot at spreading. Virality is not luck — it's the predictable result of content that exploits how attention, curiosity, emotion, and identity actually work. This skill turns that research into a repeatable process you run *with* the user, not a template you fill out for them.

## How this skill runs

This is a **conversational co-development loop**, not a one-shot generator. Work like a sharp creative partner: move one decision at a time, always lead with a recommendation, and let the user steer. Never dump a 12-part form or a finished post the user hasn't shaped.

The loop, each turn:
1. **Surface the next decision** (angle? hook? CTA?).
2. **Offer 2–4 concrete candidates**, not abstract advice — real lines they could ship.
3. **Recommend one** and say in a sentence why it's the strongest psychologically.
4. **Let them pick, mix, or reject.** Adapt and continue.

Only produce a full finished script once the angle and hook are locked. The user's taste is the final filter — your job is to give them strong material to react to.

### Step 0 — Gather just enough context

Ask only for what you don't already have (from the conversation, the project's brand memory, or context). You usually need:
- **Project / product** and the one thing this post is *for*.
- **Platform(s)** — drives length, format, hook style.
- **Goal** — awareness, engagement, leads, sales, or authority. One primary.
- **Audience** — their pain, identity, and current belief you're moving them off.
- **Truth you're working from** — the real story, data, or moment. No invented claims.

If the user is vague, propose a sharp default and ask them to correct it. Don't interrogate.

## The research foundation (the six levers)

Draw on these to generate and justify choices. The best posts **layer 3+** of them.

### 1. Hook — the first 1–3 seconds
The opening line (or first frame) decides everything; most viewers never reach line two.
- **Curiosity Loop (3 beats):** Context lean (establish relevance/pain) → scroll-stop pivot ("But…", "Except…") → contrarian snapback (a reversal that opens a gap).
- **5-element checklist (hit 3+):** pattern interrupt · curiosity gap · emotional trigger · specificity (numbers, concrete detail) · social proof.
- **Video:** bold 3–5 word on-screen text + motion usually beats a spoken hook.
- **Kills it:** "Hey guys, today…", "You won't believe this", vague stakes, or promising what the post never delivers.

### 2. Why people share (Berger's STEPPS + drivers)
Activate **2–3** consciously per post:
- **Social currency** — sharing makes the viewer look smart, insider, or in-tribe.
- **Triggers** — tie to a recurring cue (a daily habit, a season, a cultural moment) so it resurfaces.
- **Emotion** — high-arousal (awe, surprise, anger, amusement, empathy) drives sharing; low-arousal (contentment, sadness) doesn't.
- **Public** — visible actions/identities spread; private ones don't.
- **Practical value** — genuinely useful things get passed on.
- **Stories** — narrative carries the message past resistance and into memory.
- Plus: identity affirmation, FOMO/loss aversion, belonging.

### 3. Script — sequencing and pace
Hold attention by managing tension and release.
- Open with hook + an **open loop** you don't close until later.
- Structure: setup → tension/conflict → insight/resolution, *or* problem → agitation → solution.
- Front-load value/intrigue in the first 4–6 seconds; staccato sentences early.
- Weave micro-persuasion: value first (reciprocity), demonstrated results (authority), relatability (liking).
- Close the loop you opened. End on one clear, low-friction CTA.

### 4. Angle — the lens that makes it fresh
Framing changes perception. A strong angle differentiates in a crowded niche and creates a gap that demands resolution.
- Contrarian / "what everyone gets wrong"
- Identity-specific ("this is for the people who…")
- Vulnerable personal story → universal lesson
- "I tested X" / data-driven
- Overlooked mechanism / counterintuitive truth
- Pain or benefit reframe

Find one by asking: *What's the counterintuitive truth my audience avoids? What has no one in this niche said? What emotion or identity can I reframe through?*

### 5. Pattern interrupts — the orienting response
Brains habituate and auto-filter the expected. Violating the feed's pattern reallocates full attention — often the single highest-leverage move in the first 3 seconds.
- **Text:** contrarian claim, a hyper-specific number, unexpected formatting.
- **Video:** hard cut, prop reveal, framing/behaviour that breaks the genre.
- **Audio:** silence after noise, or a counterintuitive line.
- **Format break:** long text where text is rare; a carousel on a text platform.

### 6. Persuasion — turning attention into action (Cialdini, organic)
Even educational posts can position the creator as the answer — without feeling salesy.
- **Reciprocity** — give real value first.
- **Authority** — demonstrated results, specific data.
- **Liking** — relatability, shared struggle, humour, "we".
- **Social proof** — numbers, testimonials, "most people miss this".
- **Commitment/consistency** — a tiny ask ("comment YES") opens the door.
- **Scarcity** — only if *true*. Faked scarcity is a dark pattern (see ethics).
- **Unity** — "people like us do things like this."

## Generative libraries (use these to produce candidates)

**Hook patterns** — to spin 5–10 quick variants:
- "I [did unexpected thing] and [surprising result]."
- "Everyone tells you [common advice]. It's wrong, and here's what actually [works]."
- "The reason [audience] keeps [failing] has nothing to do with [obvious cause]."
- "[Specific number] [things] I wish I'd known before [milestone]."
- "Nobody talks about [overlooked thing], so I will."
- "This is for you if you've ever [specific, identity-revealing feeling]."

**Angle finder** — run the topic through 3–5 of: contrarian, identity, vulnerable-story, data/test, overlooked-mechanism, reframe. Pick the one that's freshest *and* true.

**CTA ladder** (low → high friction): react → comment a word → save → share → follow → DM → click. Match the ask to the goal and the relationship; never ask for more than the post has earned.

## Quality scorecard (audit before shipping)

Score honestly; aim for **8+/12**:
- [ ] Pattern interrupt / strong hook in first 3s or first line
- [ ] At least one curiosity gap or open loop
- [ ] High-arousal emotional trigger
- [ ] Specific detail or number (not vague)
- [ ] Social proof or authority element
- [ ] Clear, differentiated angle
- [ ] Story / narrative flow
- [ ] Practical value or social currency
- [ ] Reciprocity (real upfront value)
- [ ] One more persuasion lever, used honestly
- [ ] Natural, low-friction CTA
- [ ] **The hook's promise is actually delivered** (non-negotiable)

A high score with a broken promise is a fail, not an 11/12. Bait that doesn't pay off costs trust and gets throttled.

## Worked example: Doggo108

Doggo108 is the recurring case for adapting this skill to a real brand. When developing its content, hold its **locked voice rules** — they override generic best practice:

- **"Great weird dog walking game."** Own the strangeness; don't sand it down to sound like a normal fitness app. Weirdness *is* the pattern interrupt — it's free differentiation.
- **"Dog" and "Human", never "Player".** The dog is named first. This is non-negotiable brand voice.
- **Plain language, no jargon.** Talk to dog owners, not lawyers or growth marketers. Lead with the plain claim.
- **Show the truth.** No invented stats, no fake scarcity, no manufactured testimonials. The audience is dog people — sincerity reads, and hype repels. Real walks, real dogs, real numbers.
- **Acquisition is referral-led.** When the goal is growth, the natural CTA is share/invite a dog-walking friend, not a hard sell.

*Illustrative hook applying the framework in-voice:*
> "My dog has been quietly beating me at a game I didn't know we were playing." *(curiosity gap + dog-first + weird + open loop → reveal: the walk-scoring mechanic.)*

Notice it scores well **without** a dark pattern: the strangeness does the interrupt, the relationship does the emotion, and the CTA can stay a low-friction "tag the friend whose dog would dominate this."

For **other projects** (Giro108, HoneyDAQ, Wikispring, etc.), run Step 0 to capture *their* voice rules and audience first; the six levers are universal, the voice is not.

## Ethics — the line, not a footnote

These aren't soft suggestions; crossing them backfires algorithmically and on the brand.

- **Deliver the hook's promise.** Always. This is the whole game.
- **No invented proof or scarcity.** Fake numbers, fake testimonials, fake "only 24 hours" — all dark patterns. They erode trust faster than any hook builds it, and platforms punish them.
- **Value before the ask.** The strongest persuasion is genuine generosity, not pressure.
- **Authenticity compounds.** These techniques work *because* they're attached to something real. Bolted onto nothing, they read as manipulation and the audience feels it.
- **The human picks.** Generate strong options; the user's taste and brand judgement are the final filter.
